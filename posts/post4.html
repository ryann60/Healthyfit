What Is Strength Training? (Strength Training Explained)

Before diving into exercises, it‚Äôs important to understand what is strength training.

Strength training, also known as muscle strengthening or resistance training, is a form of exercise that uses resistance to build muscular strength, endurance, and functional ability.

Sources of resistance include:

  • Free weights (dumbbells, barbells)
  • Resistance bands
  • Weight machines
  • Bodyweight exercises

In simple terms, strength exercise challenges your muscles so they adapt and become stronger.

A strength exercise is any movement that makes your muscles work against resistance.

Common examples include:

  • Squats
  • Push-ups
  • Rows
  • Lunges
  • Deadlifts

These movements form the foundation of effective strength training exercises.

4. Improves Daily Function

From lifting groceries to carrying children, exercise strength training improves real-world movement patterns and independence.

The Deadlift: One of the Most Powerful Strength Exercises

The deadlift is one of the most effective strength training exercises for women. It targets the glutes, hamstrings, core, back, and grip strength in a single movement.

Deadlift Meaning

A dead lift involves lifting weight from the ground to a standing position using proper hip-hinge mechanics.

You may see it written as deadlift, deadlifts, or dead lifts‚Äîall refer to the same movement.

How to Deadlift Properly

Learning how to deadlift correctly is essential for safety and results.

Proper Deadlift Form (Step-by-Step)

  1. Stand with feet hip-width apart
  2. Keep the bar or weights close to your shins
  3. Hinge at the hips, not the lower back
  4. Maintain a neutral spine and lifted chest
  5. Engage your core and drive through the heels
  6. Stand tall, squeezing the glutes at the top

This is proper deadlift form and should always take priority over lifting heavier weight.

Common Deadlift Mistakes to Avoid

  • Rounding the back
  • Pulling with the arms instead of the legs
  • Jerking the weight
  • Letting the bar drift away from the body

Understanding how to properly deadlift helps prevent injury and maximize strength gains.

Deadlift Variations for Women

  • Single-leg deadlift

These deadlift exercises allow progression for all experience levels.
